Privacy Policy




Your Privacy

At Know.edu, we greatly value the privacy of our customer, be they students or teachers. No staff from Know will ever ask for your password, so do not, at any cost, give away your password.

What are we doing with your information?
Your information is used in Analytics to decide which courses are more popular, and if you are a teacher, we display some of your information on course pages so that a student may know your credentials. For both students and teachers, we use their credentials for both analytics and other purposes. Other than that, your information is generally kept safe, and our admins are not allowed to make any alterations without your consent. However, in some cases, for example, to update a teacher's contact information, we may alter your information, but this is generally done only in cases of emergency.

Are you sharing my information with other organisations?
We only share your information with select organisations, all of them being accredited and top universities that are collaborating with us. This is done for two reasons; one, to allow our students to get their courses accredited and accepted by the universities, so that their credits may be added, and two, to recruit teachers from our partnered universities to teach our courses. In either case, you may be rest assured that we are not sharing your information with any organisations except well-known and reputable universities, albeit if you wish, it can be shared with accreditation authorities as well, such as the HEC.

Are you sharing my information with any social media?
The short answer is no. While we do have official social media accounts, they are primarily for advertising (and in the case of YouTube, to upload free previews and lectures), but none of your personal or account information is shared with any of them.

How long is my information being kept?
Your account information is kept with us as long as you have your account, until or unless you delete your account, or if you are permanently banned from the website for violating our Terms of Service, in which case only your IP Address is stored (the rest of your information is made anonymous) in order to keep the ban intact. Your other information, such as cookies and browser information to give you the appropriate suggestions, is kept as long as you maintain activity on our website or as long as it is relevant following the trends of your activity on the website. This is done to tailor and suggest courses and lecturers according to your needs.



Our Privacy Promise

Know.edu is a public service. Everything we do has to benefit you, our audience. We can give you more of what you love when we understand you better. To do that, we ask you to sign in to some of our products and services. The data we collect, as such is also for your own benefit and we recommend that you sign up so that we are able to use this data effectively in two ways:

  1. To ensure that the content you are recommended is relevant to your needs.
  2. Recommend things we feel you'll be interested in.

From a public perspective, there are benefits to you using Know.edu and how your data may benefit the public as well. However, you may rest assured that this does not in any way mean we will share your personal information with the public (apart from contact details for teachers if allowed). For these reasons:

  1. Check we really are making something for everyone.
  2. To improve our content in the future depending on public ratings and receptions.
